Overfitting vs. Underfitting

In Machine Learning, the goal isn't just to memorize the data you have; it's to uncover the hidden mathematical rule (the "True Function") that generated the data so you can make accurate predictions on data you haven't seen yet.

To do this, we must configure our model and determine how long to train it. If the model is too simple, it can't learn the pattern. If it is too complex and trained for too long, it learns the pattern and the random noise, completely destroying its ability to generalize. Let's break down these three primary states of learning.

1. The Three States of Learning

Underfitting

Set the Complexity to 1 or 2 and hit Train. The model draws a straight line (or simple curve) through data that is clearly wavy. It is making a strong, incorrect assumption about the shape of the data.

  • Train Error: Very High
  • Val Error: Very High

Generalized Fit

Set the Complexity to 4 and hit Train. The green model line almost perfectly overlaps the dashed white "True Function" line. It captures the essence of the wave without twisting to hit every single noisy dot.

  • Train Error: Low
  • Val Error: Low (Matches Train)

Overfitting

Push the Complexity to 10 or 12, set Epochs to 5000 and hit Train. The model slowly contorts to hit almost every blue training dot perfectly. But look at what happens between the dots: the line shoots off into infinity, completely missing the validation data!

  • Train Error: Almost Zero
  • Val Error: Explosively High

2. The Role of Training Time (Epochs)

Notice what happens when you train a highly complex model (Complexity 12). In the first few hundred epochs, the model looks like a generalized fit. It learns the easiest, broadest patterns first.

However, as you force it to keep training towards 5000 epochs, it exhausts all the real patterns and starts dedicating its mathematical power to memorizing the noise. This perfectly illustrates why techniques like Early Stopping are essential in Neural Networks: stopping the training process before the model has a chance to memorize the noise prevents Overfitting, even if the model is overly complex.
